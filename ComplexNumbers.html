COMPLEX NUMBERS



A number of the form
\( z = a+ib \)
where \( a \) and \( b \) are real numbers is called a complex number.

The set of complex numbers is denoted by \( \mathbb{C} \).

\( \mathbb{C} = \left\{ z = a+ib \ni a,b \in \mathbb{R} , i=\sqrt{-1} \right\} \)

Note :
\(\displaystyle{ i^2=-1} \)
\( \displaystyle{ i^3={-1(i)}= -i} \)
\( \displaystyle{i^4=i^2+i^2={-1 \cdot -1}=1} \)
\( \displaystyle{i^5=i \cdot i^4=i \cdot 1 = i} \)


Suppose \( \displaystyle{z=a+ib} \) where \( x \),\( y \in \mathbb{R}\), then
We call \( x \) the real part of \( z \), we write \( x=Re(z) \)
We call \( y \) the imaginary part of \( z \), we write \( y=Im(z) \)

Conjugate Of A Complex Number

Let \( z \in \mathbb{C} \) , \( z=a+ib \)
The conjugate of \( z \) is denoted by \( \bar{z} \)
\( \bar{z} = a-ib \)

NOTE: We say that \( z \in \mathbb{C} \) is real if \( Im{(z)}=0 \).
We say that \( z \in \mathbb{C} \) is imaginary if \( Re{(z)}=0 \).


Argand Diagram



A complex number of the form \( z=x+iy \) is specified by the two real numbers \(x \) and \( y \).
To represent a complex number, we find a point in the plane whose cartesian coordinates are the numbers \( x \) and \( y \), as shown below



The diagram is called Argand Diagram. The \( x-axis \) represents the real part
while the \( y-axis \) represents the imaginary part.
By Pythagoras theorem, we have that

\( \displaystyle{ {|OP|} = \sqrt{x^2+y^2}} \)

Hence, the Modulus (or absolute value) of a complex number \( z \) is given by

\( |z| = |x+iy| = \sqrt{x^2+y^2} \ge 0\)

Polar Form Of Complex Numbers

From the diagram above, we have that

\( x=r\cos{\theta} \), \( y = r\sin{\theta}\), then
\( \displaystyle{x^2+y^2={(r\cos{\theta})^2+(r\sin{\theta})^2}} = {(r^2\cos^2{\theta})+(r^2\sin^2{\theta})} \)

\( \displaystyle{= r^2(\cos^2{\theta}+\sin^2{\theta}) = {r^2(1) = r^2}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \therefore x^2+y^2=r^2} \)

So, we have
\( z= x+iy \implies z= r\cos{\theta}+ir\sin{\theta} \)

\( \implies { z=r( \cos{\theta}+i \sin{\theta})} \)

The angle \( \theta \) is called the argument of \( z \).
We write
\( \tan{\theta} = arg(z) = \frac{y}{x} \)

\( \theta \) will have infinite number of values differing by multiples of \( 2 \pi \).
Hence, the values of \( \theta \) lying in the range \( \pi < \theta \le \pi \) is called
the principal value of the argument. This is denoted by \( Arg(z) \)

Exponential function of complex variables

Let \( z \) be a complex number

\( z = r(\cos{\theta}+i \sin{\theta}) \)

We know that

\( \displaystyle{\cos{\theta} = \frac{e^{i \theta}+ e^{-i\theta}}{2}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\sin{\theta} = \frac{e^{i \theta} - e^{-i\theta}}{2i}} \)

Now

\( \displaystyle{\cos{\theta}+ i \sin{\theta} = {\frac{e^{i \theta}+ e^{-i\theta}}{2}}+i{\frac{e^{i \theta} - e^{-i\theta}}{2i}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {\frac{e^{i \theta}+ e^{-i\theta}}{2}}+{\frac{e^{i \theta} - e^{-i\theta}}{2i}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ = {e^{i \theta}+e^{-i \theta}+e^{i\theta}-e^{-i \theta}} \over 2} \)

\( \displaystyle{ = {\frac{2e^{i \theta}}{2}} = e^{i \theta}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \therefore z = r( \cos{\theta}+i \sin{\theta}) \implies z ={re^{i \theta}}}\)


Properties Of Complex Numbers

Let \( z_1 \),\( z_2 \) be complex numbers. The following holds :

(i) \( \displaystyle{|z_1|=0 } \) if and only if \( z_1=0 \)

(ii) \( \displaystyle{ |z_1z_2|=|z_1||z_2|} \)

(iii) \( \displaystyle{\left| {1 \over {z_1}}\right| = {1 \over {|z_1|}} } \), if \( z_1 \ne 0 \)

(iv) \( \displaystyle{|z_1+z_2| \le {|z_1|+|z_2|}} \)

(v) \( \displaystyle{ arg(z_1z_2 ) =arg(z_1)+arg(z_2)} \)

(vi) \( \displaystyle{ arg \left( \frac{z_1}{z_2} \right) = {arg(z_1)-arg(z_2)}} \)

(vii) \( \displaystyle{ \left| \frac{z_1}{z_2} \right| = { \frac{|z_1|}{|z_2|}}} \)

Rules for the manipulation of complex numbers



(i) \( {a+ib} = {c+id} \)
iff \( a=c \) and \( b=d \)

(ii) \( \displaystyle{(a+ib)\pm (c+id) = (a+c)\pm i(b+d) } \)

(iii) \( \displaystyle{(a+ib) \cdot (c+id) = (ac-bd)+i(ad+bc)} \)

(iv) \( \displaystyle{ \frac{a+ib}{c+id} = {\frac{a+ib}{c+id} \cdot \frac{c-id}{c-id} = \frac{(ac+bd)+i(bc-ad)}{c^2+d^2} } } \)

where \( c-id \) is the conjugate of \( c+id \)


Examples



(1) Express the following in the form \( a+ib \)

(a) \( \displaystyle{ i^3(1+i)+i^5(3-i)+i^7(2+i)} \)

(b) \( \displaystyle{2i(1-3i)+ 3(4-i)} \)

(c) \( \displaystyle{(3+i)(4-2i)} \)

(d) \( \displaystyle{(6+2i)(1-3i)} \)

(e) \( \displaystyle{\frac{2-5i}{7+3i}} \)

(f) \( \displaystyle{\frac{1-5i}{3+2i}} \)


Solution

(a) \( i^3(1+i)+i^5(3-i)+i^7(2+i) \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {(i \cdot i^2)(1+i)+(i \cdot i^2 \cdot i^2)(3-i)+(i \cdot i^2 \cdot i^2 \cdot i^2)(2+i)}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ = -i(1+i)+i(3-i)-i(2+i)} \)

\( \displaystyle{-i+1+3i+1-2i+1 = 3} \)

(b) \( \displaystyle{2i(1-3i)+3(4-i)} \)

\( \displaystyle{= 2i-6 \cdot i^2+12-3i} \)

\( \displaystyle{= 2i+6+12-3i} \)

\( \displaystyle{= 18-i} \)

(c) \( \displaystyle{(3+i)(4-2i)} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies 12-6i+4i-2 \cdot i^2} \)

\( \displaystyle{= 12-2i+2 = 14-2i} \)

(d) \( \displaystyle{(6+2i)(1-3i)} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies 6-18i+2i-6 \cdot i^2} \)

\( \displaystyle{= 6-16i+6 = 12-16i} \)

(e) \( \displaystyle{\frac{2-5i}{7+3i}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies \frac{2-5i}{7+3i} \cdot \frac{7-3i}{7-3i} } \)

\( \displaystyle{ = \frac{14-6i-35i+15 \cdot i^2}{49+9}} \)

\( \displaystyle{= \frac{14-41i-15}{58} = \frac{-(1+41i)}{58}} \)

(f) \( \displaystyle{\frac{1-5i}{3+2i} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies \frac{1-5i}{3+2i} \cdot \frac{3-2i}{3-2i} } \)

\( \displaystyle{ = \frac{3-2i-15i-10}{9+4}= \frac{-(7+17i)}{13}} \)

(3) Let \( z_1, z_2 \in \mathbb{C} \) .Prove that

(i) \( \displaystyle{ |z_1z_2|=|z_1||z_2|} \)

(ii) \( \displaystyle{ arg(z_1z_2 ) =arg(z_1)+arg(z_2)} \)

(iii) \( \displaystyle{ \left| \frac{z_1}{z_2} \right| = { \frac{|z_1|}{|z_2|}}} \)

(iv) \( \displaystyle{ arg \left( \frac{z_1}{z_2} \right) = {arg(z_1)-arg(z_2)}} \)

(v) \( \displaystyle{\left| {1 \over {z}}\right| = {1 \over {|z|}} } \), if \( z \ne 0 \)

Solution

(i) \( \displaystyle{ |z_1z_2|=|z_1||z_2|} \)

Let \( \displaystyle{ z_1=r_1( \cos{{\theta}_1}+i \sin{{\theta}_1})} \) and
let \( \displaystyle{ z_2=r_2( \cos{{\theta}_2}+i \sin{{\theta}_2})} \)

Then \( \displaystyle{ |z_1|=r_1} \) and \( \displaystyle{ |z_2|=r_2} \)

Also, \( \displaystyle{ arg(z_1)= {\theta}_1} \) and \( \displaystyle{ arg(z_2)= {\theta}_2} \)

Now

\( \displaystyle{ z_1z_2={r_1( \cos{{\theta}_1}+i \sin{{\theta}_1}) \cdot r_2( \cos{{\theta}_2}+i \sin{{\theta}_2})}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies r_1r_2{[{( \cos{{\theta}_1}+i \sin{{\theta}_1}) \cdot ( \cos{{\theta}_2}+i \sin{{\theta}_2})}]}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies r_1r_2{[(\cos{{\theta}_1} \cos{{\theta}_2}- \sin{{\theta}_1} \sin{{\theta}_2})+i( \sin{{\theta}_1} \cos{{\theta}_2}+ \cos{{\theta}_1} \sin{{\theta}_2})]}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {r_1r_2{[ \cos{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)}+i \sin{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)}]}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{z_1z_2 = {r_1r_2{[ \cos{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)}+i \sin{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)}]}}} \)

Hence

\( \displaystyle{ |z_1z_2|= r_1r_2 = |z_1||z_2|} \)

\( \implies \displaystyle{ |z_1z_2|=|z_1||z_2|} \)

(ii) \( \displaystyle{ arg(z_1z_2 ) =arg(z_1)+arg(z_2)} \)

It follows from (i) above that

\( \displaystyle{z_1z_2= {r_1r_2{[ \cos{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)}+i \sin{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)}]}}} \)

Then

\( \displaystyle{ \implies arg(z_1z_2) = { {\theta}_1+{\theta_2}} = arg(z_1)+arg(z_2)} \)

(iii) \( \displaystyle{ \left| \frac{z_1}{z_2} \right| = { \frac{|z_1|}{|z_2|}}} \)

Let \( \displaystyle{ z_1=r_1( \cos{{\theta}_1}+i \sin{{\theta}_1})} \) and
let \( \displaystyle{ z_2=r_2( \cos{{\theta}_2}+i \sin{{\theta}_2})} \)

Then \( \displaystyle{ |z_1|=r_1} \) and \( \displaystyle{ |z_2|=r_2} \)

Also, \( \displaystyle{ arg(z_1)= {\theta}_1} \) and \( \displaystyle{ arg(z_2)= {\theta}_2} \)

Now

\( \displaystyle{ \frac{z_1}{z_2} = \frac{r_1( \cos{{\theta}_1}+i \sin{{\theta}_1})}{r_2( \cos{{\theta}_2}+i \sin{{\theta}_2})}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {\frac{r_1( \cos{{\theta}_1}+i \sin{{\theta}_1})}{r_2( \cos{{\theta}_2}+i \sin{{\theta}_2})}} \cdot {\frac{r_2( \cos{{\theta}_2}-i \sin{{\theta}_2})}{r_2( \cos{{\theta}_2}-i \sin{{\theta}_2})}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies { \frac{r_1r_2[{( \cos{{\theta}_1 \cos{{\theta}_2}}+ \sin{{\theta}_1} \sin{{\theta}_2})+i( \sin{{\theta}_1} \cos{{\theta}_2}- \cos{{\theta}_1} \sin{{\theta}_2})}]}{{r_2}^2( \cos^2{{\theta}_2}+ \sin^2{{\theta}_2})}} }\)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies \frac{r_1[ \cos{( {\theta}_1-{\theta}_2)}+i \sin{({\theta}_1-{\theta}_2)}]}{r_2(1)}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies \frac{z_1}{z_2} = \frac{r_1}{r_2}[ \cos{( {\theta}_1-{\theta}_2)}+i \sin{({\theta}_1-{\theta}_2)}]} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \left| \frac{z_1}{z_2} \right| = \frac{r_1}{r_2} = { \frac{|z_1|}{|z_2|}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \therefore \left| \frac{z_1}{z_2} \right| = { \frac{|z_1|}{|z_2|}}} \)

(iv) \( \displaystyle{ arg \left( \frac{z_1}{z_2} \right) = {arg(z_1)-arg(z_2)}} \)

From (iii) above, we have that

\( \displaystyle{\frac{z_1}{z_2} = \frac{r_1}{r_2}[ \cos{( {\theta}_1-{\theta}_2)}+i \sin{({\theta}_1-{\theta}_2)}]} \)

So

\( \displaystyle{ arg(\frac{z_1}{z_2}) = {{\theta}_1-{\theta}_2} = arg(z_1)-arg(z_2)} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies arg \left( \frac{z_1}{z_2} \right) = {arg(z_1)-arg(z_2)}} \)

(v) \( \displaystyle{\left| {1 \over {z}}\right| = {1 \over {|z|}} } \), if \( z \ne 0 \)

Let \(z=r( \cos{{\theta}}+i \sin{{\theta}}) \)

So, we have

\( |z|=r \)

Now

\( \displaystyle{\frac{1}{z} \implies \frac{1}{r( \cos{{\theta}}+i\sin{{\theta}})} } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {\frac{1}{r( \cos{{\theta}}+i\sin{{\theta}})}} \cdot {\frac{r( \cos{{\theta}}-i\sin{{\theta}})}{r( \cos{{\theta}}-i\sin{{\theta}})}} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies \frac{r( \cos{{\theta}}-i\sin{{\theta}})}{{r}^2( \cos^2{{\theta}}+ \sin^2{{\theta}})}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies \frac{( \cos{{\theta}}-i\sin{{\theta}})}{r(1)} = {\frac{1}{r} {( \cos{{\theta}}-i \sin{{\theta}})}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies \left| {1 \over {z}}\right| = {\frac{1}{r}}= \frac{1}{|z|} } \)

\( \displaystyle{= (a-ib)(c-id)} \)

\( \displaystyle{= {\bar{z}} {\bar{w}}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies \overline{zw} = \bar{z} \bar{w}} \)

(c) \( \displaystyle{\overline{({1 \over z})} } \)

now

DE MOIVRE'S THEOREM



If there are \( n \) complex numbers
\( \displaystyle{ z_1,z_2,z_3, \cdots , z_n} \)
with moduli
\( \displaystyle{ r_1,r_2,r_3, \cdots , r_n} \)
and arguments
\( \displaystyle{ {\theta}_1, {\theta}_2, {\theta}_3, \cdots , {\theta_n} } \)
then,

\( \displaystyle{{z_1 \cdot z_2 \cdot z_3 \cdots z_n } = {r_1 \cdots r_n}{[{\cos{({{\theta}_1+{\theta}_2+ \cdots {\theta}_n } )} + i \sin{({{\theta}_1+{\theta}_2+ \cdots {\theta}_n})} }]} } \)

In particular, if
\( \displaystyle{z_1=z_2=z_3= \cdots = z_n = z} \), and
\( \displaystyle{r_1=r_2=r_3= \cdots= r_n = r} \)

Then we have

\( \displaystyle{z^n = {[{r^n (\cos{n \theta}+i \sin{n \theta})}]}} \)

That is

\( \displaystyle{z^n = {[{r (\cos{ \theta}+i \sin{ \theta})}]^n}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies z^n = {[{r^n (\cos{n \theta}+i \sin{n \theta})}]}} \)

Also

\( \displaystyle{ |z^n| = |z|^n } \)

\( \displaystyle{arg(z^n) = {n[arg(z)]}}\)

In particular, If \( r =1 \)

\( \displaystyle{ {({\cos{\theta}+ i \sin{\theta}})^n} = {({\cos{n \theta}+i\sin{n \theta}})} } \)

Proof of DE MOIVRE'S THEOREM



Let \( \displaystyle{z_1 = {\cos{\theta}_1+i \sin{\theta}_1}} \)

\( \displaystyle{z_2 = {\cos{\theta}_2+i \sin{\theta}_2}} \)

\( \displaystyle{z_3 = {\cos{\theta}_3+i \sin{\theta}_3}} \)

Assuming \( \displaystyle{r_1 =1, r_2 =1, r_3=1} \)

Now, \( \displaystyle{ z_1 \cdot z_2 = {({\cos{{\theta}_1}+i \sin{{\theta}_1}})({\cos{{\theta}_2}+i \sin{{\theta}_2}}) } } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {({{\cos{{\theta}_1} \cos{{\theta}_2}} - {\sin{{\theta}_1} \sin{{\theta}_2}}})+i{({{\sin{{\theta}_1} \cos{{\theta}_2}}+{\cos{{\theta}_1} \sin{{\theta}_2}}})} } } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {\cos{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)}+i \sin{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)} } } \)

Now, if \( \displaystyle{ {\theta}_1 = {\theta}_2 = \theta} \) and \( z_1 = z_2 = z , \) then

\( \displaystyle{z \cdot z = z^2 = {\cos{({\theta}+{\theta})}+i \sin{({\theta}+{\theta})} } } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies { z^2 = {\cos{2 \theta}+i \sin{2 \theta}} } } \)

Therefore \( \displaystyle{ {({\cos{ \theta}+i \sin{ \theta}})^2} = {\cos{2 \theta}+i \sin{2 \theta}} } \)

Again

\( \displaystyle{ z_1 \cdot z_2 \cdot z_3 = {({\cos{{\theta}_1}+i \sin{{\theta}_1}})({\cos{{\theta}_2}+i \sin{{\theta}_2}})({\cos{{\theta}_3}+i \sin{{\theta}_3}}) } } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies { [{\cos{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)}+i \sin{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)} }] \cdot ({\cos{{\theta}_3}+i \sin{{\theta}_3}}) } } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {[{\cos{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)} \cos{{\theta}_3}} - {\sin{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)} \sin{{\theta}_3}}]+i{[{\sin{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)} \cos{{\theta}_3}}+{\cos{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2)} \sin{{\theta}_3}}]} } } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {\cos{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2+{\theta}_3)}+i \sin{({\theta}_1+{\theta}_2+{\theta}_3)} } } \)

If \( \displaystyle{ {\theta}_1 = {\theta}_2 ={\theta}_3 = \theta} \) and \( z_1 = z_2 = z_3 = z , \) then

\( \displaystyle{z \cdot z \cdot z = z^3 = {\cos{({\theta}+{\theta}+{\theta})}+i \sin{({\theta}+{\theta}+{\theta})} } } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies { z^3 = {\cos{3 \theta}+i \sin{3 \theta}} } } \)

Therefore \( \displaystyle{ {({\cos{ \theta}+i \sin{ \theta}})^3} = {\cos{3 \theta}+i \sin{3 \theta}} } \)

In general

\( \displaystyle{ {({\cos{ \theta}+i \sin{ \theta}})^n} = {\cos{(n \theta )}+i \sin{(n \theta )}} } \)

The Cube Roots of Unity



Lets's start by solving the equation

\( \displaystyle{ x^3-1 = 0} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies{(x-1)(x^2+x+1) = 0} }\)

\( \displaystyle{\implies{x=1}, x^2+x+1 = 0 }\)

Now, we try to solve \( \displaystyle{x^2+x+1 = 0} \)

Using the quadratic formula, we have

\( \displaystyle{x = \frac{-1 \pm \sqrt{1-4}}{2} }\)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies \frac{-1 \pm \sqrt{-3}}{2} }\)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies x = \frac{-1 \pm \sqrt{3}i}{2} }\)

\( \therefore \)we have \( \displaystyle{x=1, x = \frac{-1 + \sqrt{3}i}{2}, x = \frac{-1 - \sqrt{3}i}{2} }\)

So, the roots of the equation are \( \displaystyle{1, \frac{-1 + \sqrt{3}i}{2}, \frac{-1 - \sqrt{3}i}{2}}\)

Now, consider the square of the second root

\( \displaystyle{ \left(\frac{-1 + \sqrt{3}i}{2}\right)^2 = {\frac{1}{4}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}i-\frac{3}{4}} }\)

\( \displaystyle{\implies { - \frac{1}{2} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}i = \frac{-1 - \sqrt{3}i}{2}} }\)

We see that the result of the square of the second root equals the third root.

In the same way, the square of the third root also equals the second root.

\( \displaystyle{ \left(\frac{-1 - \sqrt{3}i}{2}\right)^2 = {\frac{1}{4}+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}i-\frac{3}{4}} = \frac{-1 + \sqrt{3}i}{2} }\)

We can represent the the roots by

\( \displaystyle{1, w, w^2}\)

Now, if we add the three roots

\( \displaystyle{1+w+w^2 = 1+[\frac{-1 + \sqrt{3}i}{2}]+[\frac{-1 - \sqrt{3}i}{2}] = 0}\)

\( \displaystyle{\implies 1+w+w^2 = 0}\)

If we multiply the three roots

\( \displaystyle{{1 \cdot w \cdot w^2} = 1 \cdot [\frac{-1 + \sqrt{3}i}{2}] \cdot [\frac{-1 - \sqrt{3}i}{2}] = 1}\)

\( \displaystyle{\implies 1 \cdot w \cdot w^2 = 1}\)


Properties of Cube Roots of Unity



(1) Sum of all cube roots of unity is zero.
(2) Product of all cube roots of unity is 1.
(3) each complex cube root of unity is the square of the other.

Useful identities

(i)\( \displaystyle{1+w+w^2 = 0} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies 1+w = -w^2} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies 1+w^2 = -w} \)

(ii) \( w^3 = 1\)


Examples

If \( w \) is one of the complex roots of unity, show that

(1) \( \displaystyle{{(1+w-w^2)^3}-{(1-w+w^2)^3} = 0} \)

recall that : \( 1+w = -w^2 \) , \( 1+w^2=-w \) , and \( w^3 =1 \)

So we have

(1) \( \displaystyle{{(1+w-w^2)^3}-{(1-w+w^2)^3} } \)

\( \displaystyle{{(1+w-w^2)^3}-{(1-w+w^2)^3}= {[(1+w)-w^2]^3}-{[(1+w^2)-w]^3}= {(-w^2-w^2)^3-(-w-w)^3} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {(-2w^2)^3-(-2w)^3} = {-8w^6 +8w^3} = {-8 \cdot w^3 \cdot w^3 +8w^3} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {-8 \cdot 1 \cdot 1 +8 \cdot 1 }= -8+8 = 0 } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {(1+w-w^2)^3}-{(1-w+w^2)^3} = 0} \)


(2) \( \displaystyle{{(1-w+w^2)}{(1+w-w^2)} } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {{[(1+w^2)-w]}{[(1+w)-w^2]}} = {(-w-w)(-w^2-w^2)} }\)

\( \displaystyle{ ={(-2w)(-2w^2)} = 4w^3 = 4 \cdot 1 = 4 } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {(1-w+w^2)}{(1+w-w^2)} = 4} \)


(3) \( \displaystyle{{(1+w)^6}= {(-w^2)^6} = w^12 } \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {w^3 \cdot w^3 \cdot w^3 \cdot w^3}= 1 \cdot 1 \cdot 1 \cdot 1= 1 }\)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {(1+w)^6}=1} \)


(4) \( \displaystyle{ {(a+b)(a+wb)(a+w^2b)}} \)

\( \displaystyle{\implies{a^3+a^2bw+a^2b+ab^2w+a^2bw^3+ab^2w^2+b^3w^3} } \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies a^3+b^3 = {(a+b)(a+wb)(a+w^2b)}} \)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies{w^12 = {w^3 \cdot w^3 \cdot w^3 \cdot w^3 = 1\cdot 1\cdot 1\cdot 1 =1 }}}\)

(6) \( \displaystyle{{(1-w)(1-w^2)(1-w^4)(1-w^5)(1-w^7)(1-w^8)}}\)

\( \displaystyle{ \implies {(1-w)(1-w^2)(1-w^3 \cdot w)(1-w^3 \cdot w^2)(1-w^3 \cdot w^3 \cdot w)(1-w^3 \cdot w^3 \cdot w^2)}}\)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {(1-w)(1-w^2)(1-w)(1-w^2)(1-w)(1-w^2)}}\)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {(1-w)^3(1-w^2)^3} }\)

\( \displaystyle{\implies {[(1-w)(1-w^2)]^3} }\)

\( \displaystyle{\implies [{1-w^2-w+w^3}]^3 = [{1+w^3-w-w^2}]^3 } \)
